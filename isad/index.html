October 22nd

International Stuttering Awareness Day

Since 1998, the stuttering community has joined together every October 22 to raise awareness, encourage support, and share their voices.

April 1988

President Reagan proclaims the first National Stuttering Awareness Week

President Ronald Reagan issues a proclamation to "enhance public awareness" of stuttering. To this day, May remains better hearing and speech month in the US.

October 2012

Judy Kuster completes 15 consecutive years of hosting annual stuttering awareness online conferences

Judy Kuster, Professor Emerita at MNSU Mankato, hosts the annual ISAD online conference for 15 consecutive years on her website, The Stuttering Homepage.

October 1998

Michael Sugarman spearheads the first International Stuttering Awareness Day

Michael Sugarman, the co-founder of the National Stuttering Project (which later becomes the National Stuttering Association), leads the first International Stuttering Awareness Day.

October 2013

120,000 visitors attend the ISAD Online Conference

Last year's online conference was seen by over 120,000 website visitors from around the world and was the first online conference hosted by the International Stuttering Association.

What is International Stuttering Awareness Day?

International Stuttering Awareness Day (ISAD) began in 1998, spear-headed by Michael Sugarman. ISAD recognizes the growing alliance between speech-language professionals and consumers, who are learning from each other and working together to share, give support, and educate one another and the general public on the impact that stuttering has on individualsâ€™ lives.
